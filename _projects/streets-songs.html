how do musicians look at the city?

START

In 2019, travel company Celebrity Cruises analyzed top-40 songs since the 1960s to find the locations that inspired them. They found that the city of New York has inspired over 160 songs, followed by London at 102. And this analysis was done by searching the phrase 'New York' in the lyrics, without any mention of the boroughs or neighborhoods of the city.

Nothing demonstrates this better than 2009's 'Empire State of Mind', which has been called the "quintessential 21st-century theme song for New York City" by the New York Racing Association.

I walked the Brooklyn Bridge last month, and I do not exaggerate when I say that this song was blasting every 200 feet.

This begs the question: what is it about New York that makes it so inspiring?

I analyzed 15 songs based on places in the city. Most of these focused on memories associated with a particular place. The two other common stories that these songs explored were starting from the bottom and "making it" in the city, and ruminations on life as a New Yorker.

Methodology

Collected street names and public spaces (parks, neighborhoods) from NYC Open Data. Searched for these using Spotify's API to get a list of songs whose names matched. After manual analysis to confirm that these are actually places in NY (ex. there are multiple Mulberry Streets across the country), picked the most popular songs and plotted them on a map in geoJSON, moved the coordinates to Datawrapper, and finished the map using Illustrator/ai2html.

For the game itself, used Javascript to build on the core game 'Wordled'. Audio was embedded in HTML elements and played using the autoplay feature.

Data journalism project for the Lede Program at Columbia.
