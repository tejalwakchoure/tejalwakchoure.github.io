START

In 2019, travel company Celebrity Cruises analyzed top-40 songs since the 1960s to find the locations that inspired them. They found that the city of New York topped the list with over 160 songs to its name, followed by London at 102. And they just looked for the phrase 'New York' in the lyrics, so this data does not include the tracks named after any famous spots or neighborhoods in the city.

No song demonstrates this city's popularity in the industry better than 2009's 'Empire State of Mind', which has been named the "quintessential 21st-century theme song for New York City" by the New York Racing Association.

I walked the Brooklyn Bridge last summer, and I do not exaggerate when I say that this song was blasting every 200 feet.

NEXT

This begs the question: what is it about this city that makes it so inspiring?

I analyzed 15 songs named after places in the city and found that most of them focused on the artists' memories associated with those places. Other common themes involved ruminating on life as a New Yorker and "making it" in the big city.

How to Play

There are 15 locations marked on this map. Each of these locations has a song named after it. The goal is to guess as many songs as you can!

You can play a 30-second clip of the song. Just like in Wordle, you then type in what you think this song is called.

The good news is that each answer is a real place, and your guesses do not have to be actual words.

Here's what those colored squares mean:
Grey = the letter is not present in the answer
Light pink = the letter is present in the answer, but not at that position
Hot pink = the letter is present in the answer at that position

Methodology

Collected street names and public spaces (parks, neighborhoods) from NYC Open Data. Searched for these using Spotify's API to get a list of songs whose names matched. After manual analysis to confirm that these are actually places in NY (ex. there are multiple Mulberry Streets across the country), picked the most popular songs and plotted them on a map in geoJSON, moved the coordinates to Datawrapper, and finished the map using Illustrator/ai2html.

For the game itself, used Javascript to build on the core game 'Wordled'. Audio is embedded in HTML elements and played using the autoplay feature.

Data journalism project for the Lede Program at Columbia.
